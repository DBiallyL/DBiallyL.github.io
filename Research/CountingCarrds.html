Privacy in Online Fandom: Counting Carrds

In this project I worked under Abby Marsh at Macalester College as part of a research team studying privacy in online fandom. In this study we examined single-serving websites called Carrds, which are commonly used in Twitter fandom as an extension of their profile.

A paper written on this research, titled "Counting Carrds: Investigating Personal Disclosure and Boundary Management in Transformative Fandom," appeared in CHI 2024, and is viewable here. I also presented on this research for my senior capstone presentation at Macalester College. Both a preprint of the paper, and the slides for my capstone presentation are linked below.
